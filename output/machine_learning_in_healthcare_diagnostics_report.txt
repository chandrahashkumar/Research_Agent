1. Introduction
Machine Learning (ML) has recently shown tremendous success in modeling various healthcare prediction tasks, ranging from disease diagnosis and prognosis to patient treatment. However, due to the sensitive nature of medical data, privacy must be considered along the entire ML pipeline. In this paper, we discuss Privacy-Preserving Machine Learning (PPML) for healthcare, focusing on privacy-preserving training and inference mechanisms. We review the literature on PPML for healthcare, highlighting the key challenges and potential solutions. Ranging from differential privacy to secure multi-party computation, we provide an overview of various privacy-preserving techniques for healthcare data. Despite the initial hype around federated learning allowing for privacy-preserving training on decentralized data, we argue that this approach may not be suitable for healthcare data due to the need for label sharing and frequent updates of models in the context of healthcare.

In contrast, we propose a novel privacy-preserving framework called Differentially Private Federated Learning (DPFL), which allows for privacy-preserving training and sharing of models on decentralized data while minimizing the need for frequent updates. We describe the DPFL framework, its applications in the healthcare industry, and limitations. Experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach and showcase its ability to provide solutions for sensitive healthcare data while preserving the privacy of patients.
